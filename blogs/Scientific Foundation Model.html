Scientific Foundation Models in Manufacturing

Author: Vispi Nevile Karkaria

Introduction

Scientific foundation models integrate large-scale data sources with domain-specific knowledge to enhance predictive capabilities in manufacturing. Unlike traditional models focused on specific tasks, these models leverage multi-modal and multi-fidelity data to improve accuracy and generalizability.

Figure: Overview of a Manufacturing Foundation Model.

Applications in Manufacturing

Foundation models are applied in various manufacturing processes, such as predictive maintenance, defect detection, and process optimization. By integrating structured and unstructured data, these models improve decision-making in production environments.

Technical Advantages

These models utilize deep learning techniques such as transformer architectures and neural operators to process complex relationships in sensor data, simulations, and experimental measurements. Their ability to learn from diverse sources enhances scalability across different manufacturing conditions.

Challenges and Future Directions

Despite their advantages, foundation models require significant computational resources and careful calibration to ensure interpretability. Future work involves improving efficiency and integrating domain-specific constraints for more reliable deployment in industrial applications.
